GradeS:
Geometry-Aware Reliability-Guided Diffusion Supervision
for Sparse-View 3D Gaussian Splatting

Abstract

Reconstructing 3D scenes from only a handful of images is severely ill-posed: standard 3D Gaussian Splatting overfits the sparse inputs and produces floaters and missing geometry in unobserved regions. We present GradeS, which augments sparse-view 3D Gaussian Splatting with geometry-aware, reliability-graded diffusion supervision. A diffusion model synthesizes guidance frames along trajectories interpolated between the input views; rather than trusting them uniformly, we estimate the per-region reliability of this guidance from scene geometry and grade its contribution accordingly, suppressing hallucinated content while reinforcing well-supported structure. This steers the optimization toward plausible, artifact-free geometry while keeping the efficiency of explicit Gaussian rendering. Across the Mip-NeRF 360 and Tanks & Temples benchmarks at 3/6/9 input views, GradeS consistently improves rendering quality over the sparse-view baseline.

Method

Given sparse input views with known camera poses, we initialize a Gaussian field from a point cloud (e.g. DUSt3R) and render guidance trajectories with a frozen base renderer. A video diffusion model refines these trajectories into consistent pseudo-observations, which are distilled back into the Gaussian field during optimization.
